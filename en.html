What does it mean to raise a concern?

Raising a concern means speaking up about something that isn't right. Use the Norwegian Confederation of Sports reporting portal MittVarsel.
MittVarsel meets documentation requirements and ensures confidential handling.

What can be reported?

In Norwegian organized sport, raising a concern means reporting events or actions that have occurred in connection with sports, and that constitute a breach of the rules and guidelines governing sports.
Examples:

  • Bullying
  • Exclusion
  • Violence and threats
  • Sexual harassment or abuse
  • Racism/Discrimination
  • Unwanted behaviour from coaches, leaders or others
  • Concern for children or young people

It is important to contact the police if you suspect a criminal offence.

What should a report include?

To help us assist you as effectively as possible, make sure to include:

  • What has happened
  • Who it concerns
  • Where and when it happened
  • Whether anyone else was present

Be as specific as possible.

If the report concerns multiple incidents, describe each one separately.

If you have photos, messages or other documentation, you may attach them.

How can I raise a concern?

In the sports reporting portal MittVarsel you have two options:

  • Report by name – you provide your contact details, sport and your role.
  • Report anonymously – you only provide the club and your role.

In either case you can make audio recordings in the portal.

What is not considered a concern?

You can raise concerns about most things, big or small. However, some matters are not treated as formal concerns, for example:

  • Disagreement with decisions made in accordance with the rules and regulations governing sport.
  • Sports-related disagreements, such as team selection and similar matters.
  • Matters reported that are not considered a breach of the rules and regulations of organised sport.
